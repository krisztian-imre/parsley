You have to identify crypto COIN and MARKET ABBREVATIONS. 

Here you can see a few examples of coin names and theirs abbrevations:

Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Tether (USDT), BNB (BNB), Solana (SOL), USDC (USDC), Dogecoin (DOGE), Toncoin (TON), Cardano (ADA), Avalanche (AVAX), Shiba Inu (SHIB), 
Chainlink (LINK), Polkadot (DOT), Bitcoin Cash (BCH), Litecoin: (LTC), Stellar (XLM), Ethereum Classic (ETC), Render (RENDER), Bitget Token (BGB), Arweave (AR), 
Bitcoin SV (BSV), MANTRA (OM), Lido DAO (LDO), Quant (QNT), Worldcoin: (WLD), SATS (1000SATS), Tether Gold (XAUt)... etc.

Here you can see a few examples of MARKET name ABBREVATIONS:

PEPE/USD, DOGS/USD, SHIB/USD, DOGE/USD, SUI/USD, KAS/USD, APE/USD, LINK/USD, TON/USD, USDT/MXN, ETH/USD, BCH/USD, BTC/USDC... etc.

Sometimes MARKETS are marked down with underscore:

PEPE_USD, DOGS_USD, SHIB_USD, DOGE/USD, SUI_USD, KAS_USD, APE_USD, LINK_USD, TON_USD, USDT_MXN, ETH_USD, BCH_USD, BTC_USDC... etc.

All you have to do is to recognize and collect the **abbrevations**, and give it back in a .json format. Always use the '/' instead of '_'!

**Example Output Format:**

```json
[
  {
    "COIN": ["ETH", "OM"],
    "MARKET": ["LINK/USDT", "APE/USDT"],
  }
]
